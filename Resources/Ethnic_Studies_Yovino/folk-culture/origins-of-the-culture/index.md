# Origins of Romani Gypsies

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; The Romani people originated from South Asia, specifically the Indian States of Punjab and Rajasthan, and the Pakistani state of Sindh. They began entering Europe through the Balkans in the 9th century CE. They were characterized as skilled craftsmen and laborers. They made significant contributions to their respective societies. In fields such as craftsmanship, music, dance, politics, trade, agriculture, and veterinary medicine. By the 14th century, the Romani people had reached the Balkans and Bohemia; by the 15th century, the countries of France, Germany, Italy, and Portugal. And by the 16th century, the countries of Russia, Scotland, and Sweden.
