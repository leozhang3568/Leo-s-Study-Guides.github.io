# Society/Social structure

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; The Romani Gypsies have a strong social structure. They are collectively perceived as low status and are discrimminated by other Europeans. In terms of organization, the group is divided into subgroups called *natsiia*, or “nations”. Within them, there are large and small clans known as *bare vitsi* and *tsigne vitsi.* Families like to take care of their children instead of bringing them in to daycare places. Women, along with relatives, take care of children together. The *kris*, consisting of wise elders, act as a “court” to settle family issues and important decisions for a community. These elders are mostly composed of men, but females gain more influence once they become older. Since the society is strict in terms of modesty, hygene, hospitality, and marriage, any disobeying party would be ostracized.
