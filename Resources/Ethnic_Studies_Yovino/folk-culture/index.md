# *Romani Gypsy*

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; How nice is it to travel all around the world, where the culture of *Romani Gypsy* scatters around. The culture originally came from Northern India, but during the 12th century, they migrated to Europe and primarily resided in Britain. If you were to visit Europe and live with a Romani Gypsy, you could enjoy their nomadic life and be on the road all the time, live in long-term, luxurious mobile homes, or live in brick houses, which are traditional to Romani Gypsies. If you are fortunate, you could live in affluent, palace-like homes, but if you are not, you might as well live in poverty. Don’t worry, though, most Romani Gypsies live in ostentious mansions.

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; While living with a Romani Gypsy, you would enjoy horse-drawn wagons, symbolizing their historic culture. You would also be introduced to their unfinished houses. Don’t criticize those for being eccentric. Their top levels are left unfinished for their children or grandchildren to finish, so each house needs to be built for several generations.
