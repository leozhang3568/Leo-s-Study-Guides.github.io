# Landscape (sacred sites and architecture)

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; The Romani Gypsies left rich landscapes where they lived. One form of architecture was Gypsy palaces. The Reformed Church of Huedin in Romania is one example. It includes decorations for multiple religions, and it had walls around them that made them a fortress because there were increasingly threats from outsiders. A more famous sacred site is Saintes-Maries-de-la-Mer in France, which is a pilgrimage for reunions of Romani Gypsies. They camp out on the streats, play around with others, and baptize their children in the Church of the Saints. This ceromony still continues from back in the Middle Ages.

&nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; &nbsp; When it comes to traditional architecture, the Romani Gypsies almost always focus on history. Their five-storied mansions consists of gold-painted columns, glass doors, and intricate geometry emblazoned on the walls. One kind of architecture, called *Kastellos*, are locatted in major Romanian cities such as Bucharest and Buzescu and are unique and not commonly   
seen.
